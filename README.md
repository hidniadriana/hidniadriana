# Hi, I'm Hidni 👋

I got into data because I liked solving problems. Five years later, I'm writing heuristic algorithms from scratch and fine-tuning language models — still the same reason, just deeper.

My career started in BI, and I've spent close to five years in it — across SEA e-commerce, digital marketing, and telecommunications. I've built pipelines, automated reporting, analysed billions of catalogue offers, and sat in rooms with C-levels, regional heads, and global brand clients to talk through revenue forecasts and business plans. The industries were different. The pressure was real. Over time I realised I kept reaching for more — more rigour, more depth, more understanding of *why* the models work, not just *that* they work. So I went back to study.

I'm currently completing an **MSc in Business Analytics at the University of Edinburgh** on a JPA Malaysia scholarship, specialising in prescriptive analytics and soft computing. My dissertation is on hub location problems in supply chain networks — building custom metaheuristic algorithms to solve instances that exact methods can't handle at scale. Before Edinburgh, I did a **BSc in Mathematics** (Minor: Data Science) and left with a Vice Chancellor's Award and a solid foundation in the maths that makes all of this make sense.

I'm building toward roles where the data work goes deep — supply chain analytics, operations research, data science — where the maths actually matters and the analysis drives real decisions. The MSc is deliberate. So is everything in this GitHub.

This is how I document the expansion — the models, the experiments, the coursework, the side projects. A record of growth, and a window into what I can actually do.
